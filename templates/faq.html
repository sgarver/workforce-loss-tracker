Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to common questions about the Tech Layoff Tracker

What is the Tech Layoff Tracker?

The Tech Layoff Tracker is a web application that aggregates and displays information about layoffs in the technology industry. We collect data from official WARN Act filings and other public sources to provide accurate, up-to-date information about workforce changes at tech companies.

Where does the data come from?

Our primary data source is the official WARN Database maintained by layoffdata.com, which aggregates WARN Act notices from all US states. We also monitor other public sources and allow community reporting of layoffs. All data is verified against official filings when possible.

How often is the data updated?

We perform automated nightly imports of the latest WARN data. The dashboard shows the last import time, and data is typically updated within 24 hours of new filings being published.

What is the WARN Act?

The WARN Act (Worker Adjustment and Retraining Notification Act) is a U.S. labor law that requires employers to provide advance notice of plant closings and mass layoffs. Companies with 100+ employees must give 60 days notice for plant closings affecting 50+ employees, or 60 days notice for mass layoffs affecting 500+ employees (or 33% of workforce).

Can I report a layoff?

Yes! We have a community reporting feature. Click "Report Layoff" to submit information about layoffs you've heard about. All submissions are reviewed before being added to ensure accuracy. We prioritize official WARN filings as the most reliable source.

Is the data free to use?

Yes, all data on the Tech Layoff Tracker is free to access and use. You can browse, filter, and export the data. We believe in transparency and making workforce information accessible to everyone.

How accurate is the data?

We strive for accuracy by using official government filings as our primary source. However, layoffs can be complex events, and some information may be preliminary or subject to change. We recommend verifying critical information with official sources.

Can I export the data?

Yes, you can export filtered layoff data to CSV format using the "Export CSV" button on the tracker page. This allows you to analyze the data in spreadsheet applications or import it into other tools.
